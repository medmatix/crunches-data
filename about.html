A Personal Sketch

My name is David York. I am working at my fourth vocation since I graduated secondary school in Canada.

I have worked as a Life and Casualty Insurance Underwiter (Head Office risk appraisal, Mutual of Omaha Canada) from which I transitioned to do a degree in Biology and Chemistry (Hon BSc., University of Waterloo), during and after which I worked as a reseach laboratory biochemist(Prof. Allan Mellors, University of Guelph). I then moved to Medicine (MD, University of Toronto, PGM Dalhousie U.) and worked as a clinician in Family Medicine and Emergency Medicine for 26 years. I also worked in Medical Administration off and on for years, during which time I acquired an MBA from Southern New Hampshire University (on campus program). I have retired from clinical and administrative medicine. I then decided to pursue work in Data Science (data analysis) since retirement from clinical medicine, and over this time I acquired the equivalent of a Baccalauriate Major in Applied Math and Statistics (University of North Carolina at Charlotte). I am in the process of completing a practicum to complete a Certificate in Data Science (Johns Hopkins University and Coursera).

I work with C++, R, SQL and Java, Javascript/HTML/CSS/PHP. I am moderately proficient using SAS/IML and I have programmed in Basic and Fortran in the past. I have used the Rockwell Arena simulation environment as well as Matlab/Octave/Scilab and somewhat less with Mathematica.

I write essays and copy, particularly on technical subjects.

My first interest is in scientific data analysis and modeling, especially of course clinical data. I am available for contract work as a freelance Data Analysis projects and Statistical Programming and Development in a Web environment and free standing (Linux and Windows environments)
