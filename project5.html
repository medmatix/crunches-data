Introduction to Medical Informatics for Practising Clinicians

In my Blog I have explored the many ways that the teaching of medical informatics is approached. A conclusion I reached was that there was a real need to reach out to practising clinicians with a course covering the middle ground between the guidelines for medical informatics curricula for residency programs and the programs to train informatics researchers and practitioners to work in that field.


Most clinicians do not want to learn to be programmers and in particular do not wish to be prepared in depth in the mathematical theory behind algorithm implementation. There is a need for an understanding of governance and management issues without necessarily making it a mini-MBA program. Knowing the pitfalls of constructing a query to get data out of a repository does not require the completing of exercises on database design and implementation. The bottom-line being - the need for understanding differs from user to user.
