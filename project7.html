An Open Movie List Database Application

My wife, Debby is a movie collector, DVDs and now Blueray. We have a pile of VHS tapes which she started collecting when I was practicing medicine in the middle of nowhere (one broadcast channel and no cable TV). It got really unwieldy finding something to watch about 15 years ago. Here is an answer to the Excel file she has been using up to now, with the potential for easy enhancements to include our own reviews and comments as well as links to IMDb and Google etc. (Believe it or not,it began life many years earlier as a dBaseII application project.)


The idea was for me to practice MySQL and the Java-MySQL interface and she'd get a full function movie list application. This is my opportunity to break it down into the a typical development process to put it into my projects. I actually started on this incarnation while I was taking a course on database management at UNCC to fill in and formalize my years of database programming. I have been puttering ever since. So, here is a growing record of my puttering, in all its understated glory.

Use Case

Description

A favorite databse development example for most texts, the needs and activities are very straight forward.

  • Adding new movies as they become part of the collection
  • Updating and Removing movie records as they are traded up or wear out (VHS especially become unplayable with time
  • Searching and retreiving movie title by different criteria.
  • Add comments and reviews for video database records
  • Associated debit to a debit account (asset or expense accounts)
  • Posting credit to an asset account with balancing entries again
  • Posting debit to a liabilities or equity account with balancing entries again
  • Display and Printout hardcopy (browsing) lists and generated reports on database items
